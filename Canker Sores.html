DISEASE DIRECTORY Oral and Dental Issues

CANKER SORES

Symptoms

Canker sores are small, painful ulcers that appear inside the mouth, often on the inner cheeks, tongue, or gums. They can cause discomfort while eating, drinking, or speaking.

Remedies

  • Rinse your mouth with a solution of salt water or baking soda to reduce irritation and promote healing.
  • Apply over-the-counter topical treatments, such as numbing gels, to reduce pain.
  • Avoid spicy, acidic, or rough-textured foods that can irritate the sore.
  • Use a soft-bristled toothbrush to avoid aggravating the sore.
  • Stay hydrated and eat soothing foods like yogurt or ice cream to ease discomfort.
