DISEASE DIRECTORY Oral and Dental Issues

CAVITIES (TOOTH DECAY)

Symptoms

Cavities, also known as tooth decay, are holes in the teeth caused by bacteria breaking down sugars and acids on the tooth surface. In the early stages, cavities often do not cause any symptoms. However, as the decay progresses, it can lead to pain, sensitivity, and discoloration.

Remedies

  • Brush twice daily with fluoride toothpaste to help remineralize enamel and prevent further decay.
  • Floss daily to remove food particles and plaque between teeth.
  • Rinse with a fluoride mouthwash to strengthen teeth and prevent cavities.
  • Limit sugary and acidic foods, which can worsen cavities.
  • Visit a dentist for proper treatment, such as fillings, if the cavity worsens.
