FOOD ALLERGIES

Symptoms

Food allergies are caused by an immune system reaction to specific proteins found in certain foods. This reaction can be severe and life-threatening, leading to anaphylaxis. Common food allergens include peanuts, tree nuts, milk, eggs, wheat, soy, fish, and shellfish. Symptoms of food allergies can vary depending on the severity of the reaction and may include hives, swelling, difficulty breathing, and anaphylaxis.

Remedies

  • Strictly avoid the food allergen that triggers your symptoms.
  • Read food labels carefully and ask about ingredients when dining out.
  • Use an epinephrine auto-injector (if prescribed) for severe allergic reactions.
  • Stay hydrated and avoid cross-contaminated foods.
  • Keep a food diary to help identify triggers.
