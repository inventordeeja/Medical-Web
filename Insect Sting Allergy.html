INSECT STING ALLERGY

Symptoms

Insect sting allergies are caused by a reaction to venom injected by insects such as bees, wasps, and hornets. Symptoms of insect sting allergies can range from mild to severe. Mild symptoms may include pain, swelling, and itching at the site of the sting. Severe reactions, known as anaphylaxis, can be life-threatening and may include difficulty breathing, swelling of the throat or face, and a rapid heartbeat.

Remedies

  • Apply a cool, damp cloth to affected areas to soothe itching.
  • Use over-the-counter antihistamine creams or calamine lotion to reduce irritation.
  • Avoid hot water and harsh soaps; opt for mild, fragrance-free products.
  • Moisturize regularly to prevent skin from drying out.
  • Wear loose, breathable clothing to avoid further irritation.
